Noisy Little Monkey

JANUARY 11, 2026 • CHILDREN'S

In the highest canopy of the forest lived a family of monkeys. They were all very lively! They laughed loudly, leaped from branch to branch making the whole tree sway, and loved playing tricks on each other.

Among them was little Kito. He learned early on that being loud and playful was the best way to live.

One morning, Kito went out on his own to explore. He was bored and wanted to find someone to play with. Soon he spotted an owl sleeping in the hollow of a tree. How cool! A new friend!, thought Kito.

He climbed up and began to shout right in her ear: "WAKE UP! WAKE UP! LET'S PLAY!"

The owl opened her eyes slowly, annoyed. "I work all night and sleep during the day..." she said in a tired voice. "Please, leave me alone." And she flew off, looking for another place to rest.

Kito shrugged and kept leaping through the branches. Soon he saw a turtle walking slowly along the forest floor. "Hey, turtle! You're so slow! Let me help you!"

Without waiting for an answer, he picked up the turtle and carried her far away, thinking it was great fun.

"Put me down!" the turtle complained. "I was heading to the lake! Now you've brought me to the wrong side of the forest!" Kito set her down and ran off, laughing.

Further ahead, he found a family of wolf pups fishing in the river. They were sitting very still, waiting for the fish to come close. Kito thought it was terribly boring. "Let's liven this up!" he shouted.

And he began jumping into the water, making noise and splashing everywhere. The fish fled in fright, and the wolf pups glared at Kito.

"You ruined our fishing!" said the oldest pup. "Now we're going to go hungry!" And they left, grumbling.

At the end of the day, old Bear was telling a story to the little animals. Everyone was sitting quietly and listening. "I know that story!" Kito interrupted, jumping into the middle of the circle. "Let me tell it! It goes like this: the elephant went there, and then..." And he began talking louder than old Bear, changing the story, making noise, not letting anyone hear properly.

The bear sighed, got up, and walked away. All the little animals left too, upset. Kito was left alone. "Hey, everyone! Come back! The story was getting good!" But no one came back.

In the following days, Kito noticed something was wrong. Every time he approached someone, the animals moved away. Nobody wanted to play with him. Sad and confused, Kito sat alone on a branch. That's when the owl landed beside him.

"You're upset," said the owl. "Nobody wants to be my friend," Kito replied, his voice very quiet.

"That happens when you only think about yourself," said the owl. "Not everyone likes playing all the time. I need to sleep. The turtle has her own pace. The wolf pups need silence to fish. The bear likes to tell his own story."

Kito lowered his head. "But I just wanted to have fun..."

"And what about the others?" asked the owl. "They deserve to feel good too. You need to pay attention to what each one needs, not impose what you want all the time. Being a friend means knowing when to play and when to show respect."

The owl flew off, leaving Kito thinking by himself for a long time.

The next day, Kito woke up different. He passed by the owl's tree and, seeing that she was asleep, continued in silence. He found the turtle and simply said: "You're almost there!"

He saw the wolf pups fishing and stayed quiet, watching from a distance. When they caught a fish, he celebrated softly along with them. When old Bear began telling another story, Kito sat down and stayed silent, listening until the end.

Little by little, Kito learned that he could still be cheerful and playful, but he could also be kind and thoughtful.
