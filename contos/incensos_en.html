Nostalgia

It took us three months to settle our mother's estate inventory. We had all gathered at her front door, our childhood home, summoning the courage to enter, imagining what feelings would surface. Inside, everything was silent, dark, dusty, inert. I was the first to catch a scent of perfume. Her perfume. The smell of a stew. Her recipe. The scent of her orchids, which should have long been dry.

Ship's Log, Stardate 45024.242

Our vessel had been infiltrated. The internal sensors proved useless against our intruder's camouflage, possibly a spy. We could not detect it by radiation, DNA sweep, motion, nor by the advanced infotic-temporal sensor on loan from the Kuls. No allied technology seemed effective. We only found its location on Deck Five a week later. Apparently, during its unexpected stay, our visitor forgot to bathe and to conceal its stench.

Intoxication

He took the garbage out, and after coming back in, carefully shut the garage door. He switched off the ambient lights and then the radio. He returned every scattered tool to its proper cabinet and dusted off the shelves. He left all the car windows open. He started the engine. He reclined and settled into the driver's seat. He waited for the carbon monoxide to spread through the air.
