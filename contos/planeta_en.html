A Planet to Sow Life

JUNE 3, 2019 • SCIENCE FICTION • MICROFICTION

She had studied the attractive brochure advertising the sale of this portion of the Milky Way, looking for some fine print, a catch, a flaw. She found none. But she was still not entirely sure whether what she was about to do was truly wise or merely rash. Well, there would be no more time to think. The Broker's message blinked on her ship's display.

He had arrived.

"Forgive me for the delay. I was stuck in traffic in Cetus," he said as soon as he teleported aboard her vessel.

"That's fine," she replied, turning her gaze from the cluster of stellar dust before her to face him.

The Broker wasted no time.

"Well, as I mentioned, this solar system is quite new. We've just created it. Before long, all this dust will dissipate and the planets will form. We expect seven to ten planets here. The sun won't be very large, but it will be stable."

"I see... a bit far from the center, much more than I imagined."

"Yes, it's on the outskirts of the galaxy and still empty, but it's a zone in rapid expansion. We're announcing this lot now, but we have several new developments planned for the coming millennia. This entire region will flourish, while the galactic center will be old and quite crowded."

"Look, I'll be honest. It's at the limit of what I can afford. I had thought of something cheaper — like buying a nebula and generating a planetary system myself," she interjected bluntly. She did not care for Brokers.

"You're right — the 'do-it-yourself solar system' is all the rage. But there's a very high risk for the inexperienced. There's a chance you won't generate habitable planets or decent suns — just those white dwarfs. We, on the other hand, have experience in this field," he said, brimming with confidence.

After a short pause, sensing his client was unconvinced, he tried a new approach.

"In addition to an excellent extended warranty, we can throw in, as a bonus, some carbon-based seeds. Very fashionable right now. You'll be able to sow the planets as soon as they're ready. Not to mention you'll be a pioneer in the region. With no competition, your seedlings could, in just a few billion years, explore other planets and proliferate on their own — you wouldn't even need to bother."

She thought it over. It was far away, and therefore affordable. Besides, it would be nice to buy something nearly ready and only have to return for the harvest. She did not want to spend time visiting and monitoring her sowings at every turn.

"All right, I'll buy it. But with the bonus... Carbon-based seeds, you said?" she said, smiling.
