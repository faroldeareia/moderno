There is no way to describe nightfall in a frozen desert. The sensation of the first night is marked within you forever. As the heat bleeds away, the cold cuts to the soul; with every breath, the frigid air drives itself to the core. The skin turns to stone, cracks, becomes part of the very ground underfoot.

No answer. Only a breathing echoed inside the tent.

A strong breeze blew in the distance, laden with fine golden grains of sand that had once been the soil of a living forest, or the bed of a coloured sea. The fact was that no life now existed over such melancholy lands. Nothing existed there anymore.

He heard about a group of hunters who had once found an entire lake, with enough water for dozens of people, when they noticed thousands of small blue beings flying like blades along the water's edge. Those diabolical beings protected the reservoir, preventing any man from approaching and quenching his thirst. Those beings had sliced the bolder hunters apart, without pity or remorse. Without doubt, terrifying stories that had frightened the young man who now, years later, could not understand how anyone had no interest in such things.

Lost in his thoughts, hours passed before a sound called him back from his reverie. He flinched. A butterfly? No, a false alarm. It was only his companion's snoring. The adrenaline would soon fade.

And so the childhood memory faded from Antonio's mind and his thoughts drifted once more. An enormous staircase appeared, silver, pointing toward a golden mountain where the wind was chained and where a peal of laughter echoed for thousands and thousands of kilometres.

The weather changed gradually as they climbed in altitude, and a haze of sand thickened little by little. Only hearing the wind, the echo, and their own breathing led them into a sad trance, as though no other purpose existed in life but to walk and walk.

The height would have revealed a beautiful landscape, were it not for the constant fog obscuring the men's vision. They were exhausted. And it was at this point on the mountain, on the edge of a physical and mental abyss, that darkness fell again. The tent was pitched: a tiny, fragile point between sharp and jagged rocks.
