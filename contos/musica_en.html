Music 3:69

FEBRUARY 19, 2019 • MICROFICTION

The Drummer

The band Calliandra would finally be at the Summer Festival, televised live, broadcast nationwide. Everyone was anxious. The night before, the drummer barely slept: he dreamed that the bass drum skin tore, that his cymbals cracked, that he had forgotten the sticks. But no one had imagined the power would fail on the day, nor that the emergency generators would break down. The drummer's thunderous solo lasted forty minutes.

The Guitarist

Xhuatn studied at a major laboratory for exploring extraplanetary civilizations and claimed to have made a revolutionary discovery. He summoned three skeptical friends, who complained they had heard similar tales before. But Xhuatn swore this time was different. He explained he had combed through excavation archives of an extinct planet, abandoned by his university as unrevealing, and found something that would change his world: a thing called a guitar.

The Vocalist

He was very good at science and technology. He knew all instruments had their own frequencies, the low rumble of a bass or the shrill of a guitar solo. He knew he could create it all on his computer. At last, no more depending on other musicians, always extravagant, irresponsible, and mouthy. He realized, however, he could not create a realistic voice. He would have to procure a throat.
