A Kingdom Surrounded by Pines

FEBRUARY 18, 2019 • MEMOIR • SHORT STORY

His kingdom was surrounded by tall pines. It was not quite his kingdom yet, for it still belonged to his grandfather, and after him would come his father. But one day, it would be his.

It needed no great walls, like the neighboring kingdoms; no moats or massive gates, for it was peaceful. Its riches were not coveted by others: there was only a stream, a lake, and a starry sky. The little heir helped feed the chickens, harvest the greens, and nail down the loose planks of the cottages.

Such was the routine in the Kingdom, until its sovereign fell ill. The nobility felt powerless and, shut inside an enormous hall, quietly discussed which futures were possible. He was a beloved King, but the best healers and sorcerers lived very far away and were anything but charitable. His father, next in the line, was the spokesman for the decision, and after much deliberation, announced they would sell the Kingdom. With the money, they would cross borders in search of the best cure possible.

Everyone knew the Grandfather-King would not live forever, but the father forgot this — as only a loving son, burdened with so many new responsibilities, could.

Today, the Kingdom no longer exists. At the time, the boy wondered how he might reclaim it. He planned the moment, wanting to be ready. He set out on his own journey: he went to study, knowing he would need knowledge; and he went to work, knowing he would need to amass wealth. The path of swords and wars was considered, but it was not very compatible with his temperament.

On this long journey, he became a man, and other conquests grew more important. And yet, of course, he still dreams of the Kingdom — though in a different way. To this day, on many of his saddest nights, he remembers his Grandfather-King, the croaking of frogs, the shooting stars; running, swimming, and climbing trees.

The man chose a new land, very distant and different, but his own. The first pine saplings are ready for planting.

***

Author's Note: This is an extremely personal story. When I was a child, my grandfather had a small farm in Ibiúna, in the state of São Paulo, but when he fell ill, my father sold the property (and I think my father never quite recovered from it). The children in the family, obviously, were not consulted on the decision. And even as an adult, I still consider it a rather bad call... Later, I grew up and now I have my own house, but I was always influenced by my childhood... I chose a place with trees, more colonial architecture, I live in front of a stream, and I hear frogs at night.
