Routine

JANUARY 5, 2024 • POETRY

While knotting his tie, stardust condensed into a planet, three moons (why not?) and a magnificent Sun.

After a few slides, tremors preceded the rise of mountains, whose blue-tinged ridgeline walled off paradisiacal beaches of high tide.

In the cafeteria queue, hundreds of tiny sailboats reached the bays — survivors of voracious monsters.

Between mouse clicks, castles rose with splendid vaults, domes adorned in bronze and gold.

Car horns blended with political intrigues, adventures of mighty wizards, journeys of solitary heroes.

While putting on his pajamas, the world dissolved, returning to stardust.
